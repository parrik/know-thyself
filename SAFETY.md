# Safety — read first

This is a tool for organizing what an LLM remembers about you. Not therapy, not diagnosis, not a friend. Limits below.

---

## Good for

- Splitting observations from interpretations.
- Showing which observations the rest rests on — so a wrong one doesn't quietly corrupt downstream claims.
- Distinguishing "restated five times" (one derivation, repeated) from "independently grounded" (multiple sources).
- Surfacing emergent insight at the intersection of patterns.
- A printable artifact you can return to when you're not clear-headed.

---

## Not

- **Not therapy.** If you're acutely distressed, dysregulated, or working through active trauma — build later, or build with someone in the room. Graph-building clarifies; it does not regulate.
- **Not diagnosis.** Novel/emergent nodes are tentative readings of limited observations. They never say "you have X" or "you are Y."
- **Not objective.** The graph reflects what the model has been told and inferred. Both partial. Working model, not ground truth.
- **Not permanent.** If a claim feels wrong, it probably is. Trust the signal over the structure.

---

## Sensitive content

Past harm, trauma, substance use, relationship difficulty — these may be load-bearing for other claims and worth preserving structurally. Flag them with `HANDLING:` directives so the model doesn't surface them casually in unrelated contexts.

You decide what gets flagged. Err on the side of flagging — easier to remove a flag than unsay something.

---

## Stop if

- The graph is producing interpretations more confident than they should be. Flag tentative or drop.
- You're reading it to feel bad about yourself. It's a map of structural claims, not a report card.
- The model asserts emergent insights without clear derivation. Ask. If it can't produce one, drop the claim.
- You're high, drunk, or in a low mood. Build when clear.

---

## Privacy

Plain-text YAML. Treat like a journal. Don't paste into untrusted systems. The file is your responsibility.

---

The graph is scaffolding, not you. An accurate partial map of some structural features of your past — not your present, future, or whole. Build it, use it, modify it, discard it when it stops helping. The method is the point, not the artifact.
